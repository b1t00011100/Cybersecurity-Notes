# Moments , Skewness and Kurtosis

## Symmetrical Distribution

- A Frequency Distribution is said to be symmetrical if the frequency on both sides of the central value (Mean) are equally distributed.
- A Symmetrical Distribution may be U-Shaped or Bell Shaped.
- In Symmetrical Distribution mean=mode=median.

## Skewed Distribution

- Skewness is used to measure asymmetry in our data. It is the measure of asymmetry that occurs when our data deviates from the norm.

### Types of Skewed Distribution

- Positively Skewed
    - In this Distribution Data is Skewed to the right
    - Mean> Mode and Median
- Negatively Skewed
    - In this Distribution Data is skewed to the left
    - Mode>Mean and Median

### Graphical Measure of Skewness

- Measures of skewness helps us to know to what degree and in which direction (Positive or Negative) the frequency distribution has a departure from Symmetry.

## Kurtosis

- **Kurtosis** is **a measure of the tailedness of a distribution**. Tailedness is how often outliers occur.
- Kurtosis is another measure of the shape of a frequency curve.
- While Skewness signifies the extent of asymmetry , Kurtosis measures the degree of peakness of a frequency Distribution.
